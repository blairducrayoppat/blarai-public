# Graduated autonomy — research groundwork (2026-07-21)

**Plain-language preamble for the Lead Architect.** This document is the homework for a decision you will make several times in the future: letting one narrow class of coordinator action run without your per-case approval. Nothing in here changes anything today — your system's ladder (`enabled_auto_classes`) is empty, every flip requires your ceremony, and this research's strongest external voices agree that is the right resting state. What this gives future sessions: your own constitution's exact requirements, what the best outside frameworks and shipped products do, the eight design questions that must be answered before the first flip, and — importantly — the places where no good answer exists anywhere and the number will have to be yours.

---

# (a) Internal state of design

**The doctrine (ADR-039, `docs/adrs/ADR-039-Coordinator-Self-Governance-Boundary.md`, ACCEPTED 2026-07-11).**

- **§2.10 (phase C5, #847)** is the ladder's constitutional text: "Individual low-risk action classes flip propose→auto behind per-class flags, each an LA governance decision, default off. Every flip gets a `DECISION_REGISTER` row **and** a per-class **Agents-Rule-of-Two re-analysis**" — the ceremony must show which leg (untrusted input / sensitive system / autonomous state change) is severed or bounded for that specific class. Rejected-alternative #12 rules out any default-on or unconditional ladder on the record.
- **§2.12.7 + §2.16** define the evidence gate: shadow mode runs full cycles with nothing surfaced; graduation requires "measured proposal precision at or above a configured threshold over N cycles," graded via a fixture-board eval suite in `evals/` that re-runs at every model swap; §2.16 additionally requires the #855 suite be graded for **induced-proposal susceptibility** (adversarial), not just classification accuracy. The named residual risk is the "weak-signal induced-proposal window" — a hostile ticket nudging a plausible proposal the operator rubber-stamps.
- **§2.17 claim discipline**: until control 7 (signed policy verification) ships, the boundary claims only "safe under expert operation" — any autonomy flip inherits that cap.
- **What ADR-039 deliberately leaves undesigned:** the threshold value and N (a "configured threshold," never chosen); the five per-class Rule-of-Two analyses themselves; the *order* in which classes flip; any **demotion/de-graduation** mechanism (the ADR designs flips up, not down — only "flag changes are operator-only" via control 4); and the full single-decision-call taxonomy (§2.15, §5 item 4, explicitly deferred).

**The enforcement code (`shared/coordinator/`).**

- `config.py:154-162` — `AUTONOMY_LADDER_CLASSES` is a closed frozenset of exactly five classes: `ticket-hygiene`, `stall-redispatch`, `readiness-refinement`, `reclassification`, `work-origination`. `config.py:207-211` — `enabled_auto_classes` defaults to the empty frozenset, resolved from `[coordinator].enabled_auto_classes` with **unknown names dropped fail-closed**; `autonomy_all_off()` (`config.py:258`) is the control-5 lock predicate asserted on fresh install.
- `output_router.py:1-63` — **two independent locks on live output**: `heartbeat_enabled=true` (its own LA ceremony) starts only SHADOW cycles; only the #855 graduation ceremony flips `shadow_mode=false`. No single flip produces operator-visible output. `shadow_mode` resolution is deliberately inverted fail-closed toward TRUE (`config.py:252-256`). Machinery-health output is never shadow-gated — structurally (no flag check exists on that path).
- `proposal_store.py:1-43` — the born-encrypted staging store is itself governed core (ADR-039 §2.1 item 10): sanctioned-API-only append, AES-256-GCM payload under the shared DEK envelope, refuse-to-start without a keystore, 7-day TTL demoting un-actioned STAGED back to DRAFT. In shadow, proposals land as DRAFT and are never promoted — the DRAFT→STAGED transition *is* the act of surfacing.

**#855's role** (`docs/research/c3-heartbeat-design-2026-07.md` §7.1): C3 built the shadow journal and router; #855 builds the grading harness over that journal (fixture-board suite per the #717 committed-baseline pattern), the measured-precision graduation, and the ceremony mechanics. Live state at authoring: the re-shadow window is open, the next #855 precision report grades guard-catch and false-refusal rates, and graduation is an LA ceremony after it (first report: `docs/governance/coordinator_shadow_precision_report_2026-07-18.html`).

**Prior external coverage** (`docs/research/coordinator-self-governance-research-2026-07.md` §7): already cites Singapore's agentic-AI Model Governance Framework (Jan 2026), NIST's autonomous-agent standards initiative (Feb 2026), ISO 42001 as the management-system shell, and Policy Cards (arXiv:2510.24383). This sweep deepens rather than repeats that layer.

# (b) External findings

*Read-level key: [read] = fetched and read (sections or full); [abstract] = abstract page only; [summarized] = passed through a fetch summarizer rather than raw full text. All external reads were performed by dispatched research subagents; read-levels are theirs as reported.*

## Q1 — Levels-of-autonomy frameworks (2024–2026)

1. **Feng, McDonald & Zhang, "Levels of Autonomy for AI Agents," arXiv:2506.12469 / Knight First Amendment Institute (2025)** [read: sections]. Five user-role levels (Operator→Observer); autonomy is a design decision separate from capability. Two directly stealable mechanisms: **"assisted evaluations"** (measure the minimum human involvement needed to clear an accuracy threshold) and **"autonomy certificates"** — an evidence-based **autonomy case** in safety-case form (objectives, arguments, evidence, operational scope, re-certification on change). *Relevance: the closest published template for what the per-class flip ceremony should adjudicate.*
2. **Morris et al. (DeepMind), "Levels of AGI," ICML 2024, arXiv:2311.02462** [read: sections]. Six autonomy levels each with a distinct risk profile; capability "unlocks" but never determines autonomy — "the choice of appropriate autonomy level need not be the maximum achievable." *Relevance: doctrinal backing that a class stays propose-only regardless of demonstrated competence until a human decides.*
3. **Mitchell et al. (Hugging Face), "Fully Autonomous AI Agents Should Not Be Developed," arXiv:2502.02649 (2025)** [read: sections]. Grades agents by who controls program flow; risk rises monotonically with ceded control; rejects the top level ("model creates & executes new code") outright. *Relevance: supports a permanent structural ceiling — "agent mints new action classes" is categorically off the ladder.*
4. **Calvert, "Operationalisation of Meaningful Human Control," Sci. & Eng. Ethics 31:27 (2025)** (building on Santoni de Sio & van den Hoven 2018) [read: sections; 2018 root abstract-only]. Converts MHC's **tracking/tracing** conditions into nine operational principles; explicitly concedes the literature has **no quantitative criteria** for when more autonomy is justified. *Relevance: "tracing" is the academic name for the LA-present ceremony — one identifiable human who understood and accepted responsibility per flip; thresholds must be defined locally.*
5. **Cihon et al., "Measuring AI Agent Autonomy: code inspection," arXiv:2502.15212 (NeurIPS SoLaR)** [read: abstract+sections]. Scores autonomy by static inspection of orchestration code (impact × oversight), making autonomy claims auditable pre-deployment. *Relevance: the per-class flag's wiring diff is itself first-class ceremony evidence — matches structural-absence doctrine.*
6. **Weber & Taneja, "The Digital Apprentice," arXiv:2606.04321 (2026)** [abstract-only; trust caveat: affiliations unstated, position paper]. Per-skill autonomy state machine: promotion needs empirical evidence *plus* recorded human authorization; **demotion is asymmetric — automatic on quality degradation, no human required**. *Relevance: the only source found matching the BlarAI pattern one-to-one; contributes the auto-demotion idea.* Companion warning: Grunde-McLaughlin et al. (Microsoft), arXiv:2602.16844 — better oversight UIs raised human *confidence* without raising error-detection *accuracy*; "the LA reviewed the journal" is weaker evidence than measured precision.

## Q2 — Industry permission-system practice

1. **Claude Code permissions (code.claude.com/docs/en/permissions)** [read: full page]. Deny→ask→allow rules with tool-specifier syntax; **`dontAsk` mode auto-denies everything not pre-approved via allow rules — a shipped propose-only posture**; a deny at any settings level cannot be re-allowed below it; grant persistence differs by action class (Bash grants durable per-repo, edit grants session-only); PreToolUse hooks can deny but never override a declarative deny. *Relevance: `dontAsk` + per-class allowlist is precisely a propose→auto ladder in production today, including the durable-vs-session grant distinction.*
2. **Anthropic computer-use guidance** [read: full page, security block verbatim]. Human confirmation keyed to **consequence class** ("meaningful real-world consequences and any tasks requiring affirmative consent"), plus VM isolation and domain allowlists. *Relevance: the auto-vs-ceremony cut drawn by consequence, not by tool.*
3. **OpenAI ChatGPT agent Watch Mode (deploymentsafety.openai.com)** [read: sections; Operator system card 403'd]. Three cumulative supervision tiers — confirmation before significant actions, Watch Mode (pause when the user stops supervising, in sensitive contexts), takeover mode — selected by context class. *Relevance: shipped precedent for graduated oversight rungs within one approved capability.*
4. **Renovate automerge (docs.renovatebot.com)** [summarized, quoted lines]. `automerge: false` default; auto-execution gated on **passing status checks** (skipping requires explicit `ignoreTests: true`), **severity class** (`matchUpdateTypes: ["minor","patch"]`, pre-1.0 excluded, majors stay human), and **aging** (`minimumReleaseAge` soak period); all per-package. *Relevance: the most mature full analog — per-class flags + observable evidence gate + severity taxonomy + soak window.*
5. **Dependabot + branch protection (docs.github.com)** [summarized]. The bot never merges itself; a workflow classifies via typed metadata and *queues* the merge; platform-enforced required checks execute it. *Relevance: eligibility decision and evidence-gate enforcement live in separate, independently-owned layers — the proposer never enforces its own graduation conditions (matches author≠verifier and the #855 split).*
6. **MCP ToolAnnotations (spec 2025-06-18 + canonical schema.ts)** [read: full]. Four-axis vocabulary — `readOnlyHint` / `destructiveHint` / `idempotentHint` / `openWorldHint` — with the spec's own warning that annotations are hints, never a security boundary. *Relevance: a ratified classification vocabulary for the five ladder classes; self-declared class ≠ enforcement layer.*

## Q3 — Governance frameworks (AIGP-tagged)

1. **NIST AI RMF 1.0 (NIST AI 100-1)** [read: primary text, core tables]. **MANAGE 1.1** makes deploy/proceed a documented determination; **MAP 1.5/GOVERN 1.3** require documented risk tolerance; MANAGE 1.3/1.4 make risk *acceptance* and residual risk documented acts. *Relevance: a per-class ceremony is a MANAGE 1.1 determination at fine grain; the DECISION_REGISTER row is the risk-acceptance artifact.* **AIGP: Domain II names the RMF to subcategory level.**
2. **NIST AI RMF oversight/revocation subcategories** [same read]. **GOVERN 3.2** (roles for human-AI configurations), **MAP 3.5** (documented oversight processes), **MANAGE 2.4** ("mechanisms... to supersede, disengage, or deactivate AI systems that demonstrate performance... inconsistent with intended use"), MANAGE 4.1 (post-deployment monitoring incl. override), GOVERN 1.7 (decommissioning). *Relevance: graduation without a documented reverse gear is non-conformant by MANAGE 2.4.* **AIGP: "human-AI configuration" is exam vocabulary.**
3. **NIST Generative AI Profile (NIST AI 600-1)** [read: primary text, action IDs]. **MG-2.4-004**: "Establish and regularly review specific criteria that warrants the deactivation... in accordance with set risk tolerances"; MG-2.4-002: escalation when deactivation criteria are met "for a particular context of use or for the GAI system as a whole"; names automation bias as a first-class risk. *Relevance: "per context of use" is the standards twin of per-action-class grain — each flipped class should carry written, periodically reviewed deactivation criteria.*
4. **EU AI Act Arts. 14 / 12 / 26** [read: full article pages via artificialintelligenceact.eu mirror; EUR-Lex not cross-checked]. Art. 14: oversight "commensurate with the risks, level of autonomy and context of use"; the human must be able to disregard/override/reverse output and "interrupt the system through a 'stop' button"; Art. 12: lifetime automatic logging; Art. 26: deployers assign oversight to persons with "competence, training and authority." Legal caveat: binds high-risk systems on the EU market — for BlarAI a design blueprint, not an obligation. **AIGP: the most-named law in the BoK.**
5. **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** [standard text NOT read — paywalled; claims rest on standards-body overviews]. Certifiable AI management system: documented impact assessments with periodic reassessment, Statement of Applicability over Annex A controls (human oversight is a normative control area), lifecycle change controls. *Relevance: an autonomy flip is a controlled change — re-run the impact assessment, update the SoA, record the loop.*
6. **IAPP AIGP Body of Knowledge 2025 (official PDF)** [read: primary text, all 9 pages]. Domain IV requires "continuous monitoring," documented post-market monitoring plans, and "a policy and controls to **deactivate or localize an AI system**... due to... performance issues." The 2025 BoK cites **ISO 42005 (AI system impact assessment)** in its core-standards line. *Relevance: the graduation paperwork doubles as AIGP portfolio evidence verbatim; name artifacts using "AI system impact assessment" vocabulary.*

## Q4 — Design patterns with evidence for graduation

1. **IDx-DR / Abràmoff et al., npj Digital Medicine (2018) + FDA De Novo DEN180001** [summarized; FDA PDF located, NOT read]. First FDA authorization of an AI diagnosing **without clinician interpretation** rested on **pre-specified** endpoints — sensitivity >85%, specificity >82.5% against an independent human reading center — measured at 87.2% / 90.7% (n=819 analyzable, 10 sites). *Relevance: the strongest external analogue for #855 — pre-declared thresholds, fixed N, graded against a* stronger *human reference, declared before measurement.*
2. **AWS SageMaker shadow tests (developer guide)** [read: full]. Shadow variant receives sampled replicated traffic, returns nothing to callers; **default duration 7 days, min 1 hour, max 30 days**; promotion is always a deliberate human action after dashboard comparison. *Relevance: vendor-productized anchor for shadow-window sizing and keeping promotion a ceremony.*
3. **Google SRE Workbook ch. 16, "Canarying Releases"** [summarized, quoted passages]. Multi-stage ramps, stack-rank to ≤~a dozen harm-mapped metrics, roll back on degradation; **deliberately declines universal numeric thresholds**. *Relevance: precedent for a staged ladder with per-stage metrics and rollback-as-default-failure-path.*
4. **OWASP AI Agent Security Cheat Sheet + OpenTelemetry GenAI agent spans** [read: summarizer with exact fields]. OWASP audit fields for high-risk agent actions: action classification, risk score, **authorization outcome, approval identifier**, execution result, **policy version**, identity/timestamps, tamper-evidence, approvals bound to the exact action (actor, tool, target, parameters, expiry); four risk tiers with **auto-approval only at the lowest**. OTel agent-span attributes are Development-status — don't pin to them. *Relevance: a checkable schema for what each shadow-journal row and post-flip action record must carry.*
5. **EU AI Act Art. 14(4)(d)-(e)** [read: operative text]. Override/reverse and safe-halt stop capability are design requirements that **persist after go-live**. *Relevance: the ladder must be bidirectional by construction — the propose path and kill switch stay wired for auto-flipped classes.*
6. **Zheng et al., "Judging LLM-as-a-Judge," NeurIPS 2023, arXiv:2306.05685** [abstract-level numbers]. GPT-4-judge reached **>80% agreement with humans — matching human-human agreement (~81%)** — presented as the explicit justification for delegation. *Relevance: the canonical "agreement-parity" precedent — the principled threshold is parity with the human ceiling, which means #855 should also measure the LA's own self-consistency to know what parity means.*

# (c) Synthesis — the eight design questions a future session must answer

1. **What is each class's graduation threshold, and what does it reference?** Absolute precision bar, or parity with measured LA self-consistency? (Zheng: agreement-parity as the principled anchor; IDx-DR: thresholds **pre-specified before measurement** — declare and register the #855 threshold before the grading window opens, or it's a target fitted to the data; SRE + Calvert: no universal number exists.)
2. **What is the evidence window (N cycles / days), and against which reference judge?** (SageMaker: 7–30 days productized; IDx-DR: fixed N against a *stronger* reference; Grunde-McLaughlin: journal-review confidence is not accuracy — grading must be outcome-checked.)
3. **Is demotion automatic or ceremonial — and who executes it, given control 4?** The literature is unanimous that de-escalation must exist (NIST MANAGE 2.4; GenAI Profile MG-2.4-004 written deactivation criteria per context of use; EU Art. 14 stop button; canary auto-rollback; Weber & Taneja asymmetric auto-demotion). But ADR-039 control 4 forbids any inside-write to `[coordinator]` config — an auto-demotion that flips `enabled_auto_classes` is a config write the coordinator surface must not perform. Resolve the tension: e.g. a deterministic launcher-side (outside-the-coordinator-surface) demotion mechanism, or a runtime "suspended" state distinct from config. **The single most novel design question — no standards-grade pattern exists.**
4. **What is the per-class evidence artifact the ceremony adjudicates?** (Feng's **autonomy case** in safety-case form — subsumes ADR-039's Rule-of-Two re-analysis; Cihon: the flag-wiring diff as inspectable evidence; NIST MANAGE 1.1/1.3; use ISO 42005 "AI system impact assessment" vocabulary for AIGP portfolio value.)
5. **What does the post-flip audit record contain, and where does it live?** (OWASP field list; EU Art. 12; OTel unstable. The briefing ledger and shadow journal exist; the gap is the **auto-executed-action** record schema and its linkage to the ceremony's DECISION_REGISTER row.)
6. **How does the oversight surface strengthen as classes flip?** (EU Art. 14 proportionality; OpenAI cumulative tiers.) Each flip should *add* monitoring — a live per-class precision line, a visible last-N-actions feed — not just remove approvals; live precision keeps being measured against post-hoc LA spot-checks (NIST MEASURE 2.4 / MANAGE 4.1).
7. **Where is the ladder's structural ceiling stated?** (Mitchell: "the model mints new action classes" must be categorically impossible.) The code implements this (closed `AUTONOMY_LADDER_CLASSES` frozenset, unknown names dropped fail-closed) — but no doctrine sentence *states* the closed-registry property as constitutional. A future ADR amendment should.
8. **In what order do the five classes flip, ranked by what?** (Renovate/Dependabot severity classes; MCP's four axes; OWASP auto-approve-only-lowest.) Rank by reversibility and blast radius before scheduling any ceremony — plausibly `ticket-hygiene` (additive, idempotent, closed-world) first and `work-origination` last — but the ranking is itself a design deliverable with a per-class Rule-of-Two worksheet.

Cross-cutting confirmation: the Dependabot/branch-protection split (proposer never enforces its own graduation gate) independently validates the existing #855 architecture — deterministic harness grades, coordinator is graded, LA decides.

# (d) Honesty section — what could not be sourced well

- **No empirical study quantifies when an autonomy transition is safe.** Nobody publishes a validated precision threshold, cycle count, or statistical test justifying propose→auto. The argument structure exists (safety cases); the numbers will be locally defined.
- **Automatic demotion criteria** have no standards-grade source — canary auto-rollback is the nearest analogue; the one exact match (Weber & Taneja) is an abstract-only position paper with unstated affiliations.
- **Paywalled, not read:** ISO/IEC 42001 and 23894 full texts, SAE J3016 primary text, IEEE 7000-series. FDA DEN180001 review PDF located but not read.
- **OpenAI primary sources partially blocked** (Operator system card 403'd); Google/Microsoft browser-agent guardrail primary docs not covered.
- **Proprietary numbers** (fraud-industry champion-challenger thresholds, SOC false-positive rates) excluded rather than cited at survey grade.
- **Relay caveat:** external reads performed by dispatched subagents; read-levels as reported; summarizer-mediated items flagged. Internal citations read directly on disk by the authoring session.
